Chess Pieces

The King

The whole set consist of two kings of different colors. The aim of the game is to keep the king from getting trapped with no escape square. A position called check mate. Every other piece has an allegience to protect the king, else, the game is lost to the opponent. The king moves one square in any direction except when castling where it moves two steps to either right or left depending on the chosen castle side.

The Queen

This is the most powerful piece on the board. It moves in both diagonal and horizontal directions as long as there is no obstacle on the path. It captures any opposing piece on its path.

The Bishop

The bishop moves in a diagonal path punishing any oppossing piece in its path by capturing them

The Rook

This piece takes a invasive and deadly approach. It moves in a horizontal path from the square it resides on There being two rooks, it is advisable to align your rooks so as to form a formidable defence/attack structure

The Knight

The Knight takes a subtle yet lethal strategy. Moving by skipping two squares and settling on the next square

The Pawn

The pawn is definitely the most restricted piece in movement. It can move forward two steps in its first move after which it moves forward only once. However, when strategically placed on the board, the pawns can be your best guardians or attackers.

Strategies THat you can use as a beginner

Center Development

Always aim to control the center of the board. you can do this with any of your pieces but mostly with the knights and pawns

Develop Your Major Pieces

Major pieces are the bishops, rooks and queen. you have to place them in areas where they have more attack impact.

Early Castle

Castle within ten moves into the game. This puts the king at a better protected position It also introduces/ develops your rook bringing it to play.

Think of a better Move

when you find a good move, think of the better to best move. This way, all your moves are calculated and can be accounted for.
